## Overview

Peloton expanded capacity during pandemic demand, then faced reopening, inventory pressure, layoffs, and a sharp valuation collapse.

## Management Problem

How should companies distinguish temporary demand spikes from durable shifts?

## Why It Matters

Peloton is a strong case for studying forecasting under extreme external shocks.

## Debate Question

> How should companies respond to sudden demand spikes, and when is expansion a mistake?

## Evidence to Discuss

- Pandemic behavior was not necessarily permanent demand.
- Capacity commitments reduced flexibility.
- The case shows the cost of scaling before demand uncertainty resolves.
