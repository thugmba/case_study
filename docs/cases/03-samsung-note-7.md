## Overview

Galaxy Note 7 batteries overheated and caught fire, forcing recalls, flight bans, and the discontinuation of a flagship product.

## Management Problem

Should Samsung have killed the Note brand entirely, or separate the failed product from the broader brand?

## Why It Matters

Samsung's recovery makes the case useful for studying crisis response after a visible product-safety failure.

## Debate Question

> Should Samsung have killed the Note brand entirely, and how did it recover trust?

## Evidence to Discuss

- The first recall did not fully solve the safety problem.
- Flight bans turned a product defect into a public trust crisis.
- The response required both technical root-cause analysis and reputation repair.
