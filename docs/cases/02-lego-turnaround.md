## Overview

LEGO nearly collapsed after expanding into too many product lines and non-core adjacencies. The turnaround cut complexity and rebuilt innovation around the core brick system.

## Management Problem

Should management cut products and reduce variety even when some lines appear successful?

## Why It Matters

The case shows how focus can increase creativity by giving design and operations clearer constraints.

## Debate Question

> When should companies cut successful product lines, and how do they know they have over-extended?

## Evidence to Discuss

- Product variety created hidden costs in molds, parts, inventory, and forecasting.
- A clearer identity around the brick system improved coordination.
- Fan communities became a source of learning rather than a distraction.
