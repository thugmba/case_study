## Overview

OpenAI's board removed Sam Altman, employees and Microsoft pushed back, and Altman returned days later with a changed board structure.

## Management Problem

Can a nonprofit-style board control a high-growth AI company with major commercial partners?

## Why It Matters

The case is useful because mission governance, investor expectations, employee power, and partner dependence collided in public.

## Debate Question

> Was the board right to fire Altman, and is OpenAI's structure workable?

## Evidence to Discuss

- Board authority existed legally but was fragile organizationally.
- Employee and partner reactions changed bargaining power.
- The case tests governance designs for frontier technology.
