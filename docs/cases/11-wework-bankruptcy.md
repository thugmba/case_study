## Overview

WeWork's IPO collapsed after investors scrutinized losses, related-party transactions, governance risks, and the gap between tech valuation and real-estate economics.

## Management Problem

What governance and valuation checks should constrain charismatic founders?

## Why It Matters

The case exposes how narrative, growth, and founder charisma can overwhelm unit economics and governance discipline.

## Debate Question

> How did sophisticated investors get fooled, and what governance failed?

## Evidence to Discuss

- The S-1 made governance and losses more visible.
- Real-estate obligations did not behave like software economics.
- Founder control and related-party dealings damaged trust.
